Lesson 45 | Overview Concentration Calculations

1.34C
understand how to carry out calculations involving amount of substance, volume, and concentration (in mol/dm3) of solution

In this lesson, students will learn to:

  • state the meaning of the term ‘concentration’
  • calculate concentration in g/dm3 and mol/dm3
  • calculate amount of substance, volume, and concentration (in mol/dm3) of solution.

Students should already be familiar with how solutions are formed by a solute dissolving in a solvent from their work in Lesson 2.

  • mass concentration = \(\frac{\mathrm{mass}\;\mathrm{of}\;\mathrm a\;\mathrm{solute}}{\mathrm{volume}\;\mathrm{of}\;\mathrm{solution}}(\mathrm g/\mathrm{dm}^3)\)

  • molar concentration = \(\frac{\mathrm{moles}\;\mathrm{of}\;\mathrm a\;\mathrm{solute}}{\mathrm{volume}\;\mathrm{of}\;\mathrm{solution}}(\mathrm{mol}/\mathrm{dm}^3)\)

Pearson Edexcel International GCSE (9–1) Chemistry Student Book: pp. 66–71


There are no practicals in this lesson.

Other equipment required for this lesson

  • A variety of pieces of glassware with measurement scales (for example, 10 cm3 measuring cylinder, 50 cm3 measuring cylinder, 250 cm3 conical flask), aqueous sodium chloride (350 cm3, 5 g/dm3) or water (Starter).
  • The units of concentration may be confusing for some students as they are unlikely to have encountered them before. Try to always read out the unit fully, as words, not just letters. For example, g/dm3 should be ‘grams per decimetre cubed’ not ‘g per dm cubed’ as students need to become familiar with the unit.
  • Breaking calculations down into multiple steps will help students understand what is happening.

Resources

Learning objectives

Learning objectives

In this lesson, students will learn to:

  • state the meaning of the term ‘concentration’
  • calculate concentration in g/dm3 and mol/dm3
  • calculate amount of substance, volume and concentration (in mol/dm3) of solution.

Key definitions

Key definitions

  • mass concentration = \(\frac{\mathrm{mass}\;\mathrm{of}\;\mathrm a\;\mathrm{solute}}{\mathrm{volume}\;\mathrm{of}\;\mathrm{solution}}(\mathrm g/\mathrm{dm}^3)\)

  • molar concentration = \(\frac{\mathrm{moles}\;\mathrm{of}\;\mathrm a\;\mathrm{solute}}{\mathrm{volume}\;\mathrm{of}\;\mathrm{solution}}(\mathrm {mol}/\mathrm{dm}^3)\)

Teaching session 1

Concentration

  • A solution is made when a soluble substance, known as the solute, is dissolved in a solvent, such as water.

  • Increasing the proportion of the solvent will lead to the solution being more dilute.
  • Increasing the proportion of solute will lead to a more concentrated solution, until eventually it is saturated, and no more solute can dissolve.

Molar concentration

  • Molar concentration is when 1 mole of solute dissolved in 1 dm3 of solution makes a solution with a concentration of 1 mol/dm3.

Converting units

  • Use the following technique to convert between cm3 and dm3.
  • Use the following technique to convert between mol/dm3 and g/dm3.
  • Use the Mr because mass = Mr × mol.

Calculating concentrations

Calculate the concentration of the following solutions:

  • 0.5 moles in 3 dm3 of solution
  • 3 moles in 200 cm3 of solution
  • 0.25 moles in 0.03 dm3 of solution.

Calculating concentrations

Calculate the concentration of the following solutions:

  • 0.5 moles in 3 dm3 of solution = 0.5 ÷ 3 = 0.167 mol/dm3
  • 3 moles in 200 cm3 of solution= 3 ÷ 0.2 = 15.0 mol/dm3
  • 0.25 moles in 0.03 dm3 of solution = 0.25 ÷ 0.03 = 8.33 mol/dm3

Reacting volumes

Worked example 1

What volume of 3.00 mol/dm3 NaOH reacts fully with 25.0 cm3 of 0.750 mol/dm3 H2SO4?

2NaOH(aq) + H2SO4(aq) → Na2SO4(aq) + 2H2O(l)

Worked example 1

What volume of 3.00 mol/dm3 NaOH reacts fully with 25.0 cm3 of 0.750 mol/dm3 H2SO4?

2NaOH(aq) + H2SO4(aq) → Na2SO4(aq) + 2H2O(l)

Answer

  • The volume and concentration of H2SO4 are present in the question, so this is the starting point.
    The volume needs to be converted into dm3.

    Step 1: moles = concentration × volume

 moles of H2SO4 = 0.750 × 0.025

= 0.01875 mol

Note that this answer is not to 3 significant figures – until the final answer, all answers should be kept as full calculator values.

Worked example 1

What volume of 3.00 mol/dm3 NaOH reacts fully with 25.0 cm3 of 0.750 mol/dm3 H2SO4?

2NaOH(aq) + H2SO4(aq) → Na2SO4(aq) + 2H2O(l)

Answer

  • Now the moles of NaOH are known, the volume of NaOH can be determined.

Step 3:     volume = moles ÷ concentration

volume of NaOH = 0.0375 ÷ 3.0

= 0.0125 dm3

The volume of H2SO4 was given in cm3, so give your final answer in cm3 also:

volume of NaOH = 0.0125 dm3 = 12.5 cm3

Exam-style question

Calculate the concentration, in g/dm3, of a solution of nitric acid, HNO3, with a concentration of 0.4 mol/dm3. [3 marks]

Ar values: N = 14, H = 1, O = 16

Mr HNO3 = 1 + 14 + (3 × 16) = 63 [1 mark]

concentration = 63 × 0.4 mol/dm3 [1 mark]

concentration = 25.2 g/dm3 [1 mark]

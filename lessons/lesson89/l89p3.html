Learning objectives

Learning objectives

In this lesson, students will learn to:

  • explain the term position of equilibrium
  • explain how a change in temperature will affect the position of equilibrium
  • explain how a change in pressure may affect the position of equilibrium
  • explain the change in position of equilibrium, if any, as temperature and pressure are altered.

Key definitions

Key definitions

  • position of equilibrium: a reference to the proportion of the various things in an equilibrium mixture; if the position of equilibrium of a reaction lies towards the right, the equilibrium mixture contains a higher proportion of products than reactants
  • pressure: the force acting on something per unit area; pressure has the SI unit of Pa (N/m2) and is caused by molecules hitting the wall of their container

Teaching session 2

Effect of changing conditions on the position of equilibrium

Changing temperature

Increasing temperature

  • Equilibrium position shifts in the direction of the endothermic reaction.

Decreasing temperature

  • Equilibrium position shifts in the direction of the exothermic reaction.

Changing pressure

Increasing gas pressure

  • Equilibrium position shifts in the direction that produces fewer gas molecules.
  • Producing fewer gas molecules reduces pressure.

Decreasing gas pressure

  • Equilibrium position shifts in the direction that produces more gas molecules.
  • Producing more gas molecules increases pressure.

  2. As the temperature is decreased, the equilibrium shifts to the right so the colour gets lighter. This changes brown NO2 molecules into colourless N2O4 molecules as this is the exothermic direction, which increases the temperature.
    If the pressure is increased, the equilibrium position shifts right to form more N2O4 as this decreases the pressure again. When this happens, the colour gets lighter as N2O4 is colourless.

Challenge

Question 1

Evaluate the use of high temperatures and pressures for industrial reactions.

High temperatures increase the rate of attainment of equilibrium and rate of reaction but may reduce the equilibrium yield. They may also be too expensive to be worthwhile. The temperature chosen may be a compromise between yield and rate.

High pressures increase the rate of attainment of equilibrium and rate of reaction. In reactions involving reacting gases it may reduce the equilibrium yield, and it may be too expensive to be worthwhile. The pressure chosen may be a compromise between yield and rate.

Exam-style question

  • The formation of methanol from carbon monoxide and hydrogen is a reversible reaction that is exothermic in the forward direction. Explain the effect on the position of equilibrium of increasing the temperature of the reaction.

Answer

  • The formation of methanol from carbon monoxide and hydrogen is a reversible reaction that is exothermic in the forward direction. Explain the effect on the position of equilibrium of increasing the temperature of the reaction.
  • Increasing the temperature shifts the equilibrium to the left as this is the endothermic direction and so reduces the temperature.

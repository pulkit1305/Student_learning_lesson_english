Learning objectives

Learning objectives

In this lesson, students will learn to:

  • state the meaning of the term ‘fuel’
  • state that full (complete) combustion of hydrocarbons releases carbon dioxide and water
  • state the conditions leading to incomplete combustion
  • explain why carbon monoxide forms in incomplete combustion
  • explain why carbon monoxide is toxic.

Key definitions

Key definitions

  • complete combustion: occurs when a hydrocarbon burns in sufficient oxygen carbon dioxide and water are formed as products
  • incomplete combustion: occurs when a hydrocarbon burns in insufficient oxygen; water is still formed as a product, but carbon monoxide and carbon are formed instead of carbon dioxide

Hydrocarbons as fuels: Complete combustion

hydrocarbon + oxygen → carbon dioxide + water

A fuel is a substance which, when burned, releases heat energy. Therefore, the various fractions obtained from crude oil can be used as fuels.

A good fuel is one which:

  • is safe to use
  • releases a lot of heat energy
  • is easy to ignite
  • is convenient to store.

Hydrocarbons as fuels: Incomplete combustion

Incomplete combustion products

Incomplete combustion occurs when there is a limited supply of oxygen.

Water is still produced, but carbon and carbon monoxide are formed instead of carbon dioxide:

hydrocarbon + oxygen → carbon + carbon monoxide + water

In poorly maintained or unventilated appliances like boilers and heaters, although some carbon dioxide may also still be produced, it is possible for incomplete combustion to occur.

Carbon

This wall has been blackened by soot from the exhaust pipe of an old boiler.

Carbon monoxide

Carbon monoxide can sometimes be fatal.

Safety

Carbon monoxide detectors are used in homes that use natural gas for heating or cooking.

Learning objectives

Learning objectives

In this lesson, students will learn to:

  • explain why nitrogen is inert, but that it will react in an engine
  • explain how sulfur dioxide can be formed during combustion
  • state how sulfur and nitrogen oxides form acid rain
  • describe some of the consequences of acid rain.

Key definitions

Key definitions

  • acid rain: rain which has a pH of less than about 5.6; it is caused when water and oxygen in the atmosphere react with sulfur dioxide to produce sulfuric acid or with various oxides of nitrogen, NOx, to give nitric acid
  • impurities: unwanted substances found mixed into a useful substance
  • pollutant: a substance that harms living organisms when released into the environment
  • scrubbing: removing pollutant gases from the gases produced in a combustion reaction
  • weathering: when rocks are broken up by physical, chemical or biological processes

Acid rain

Producing sulfur dioxide

  • As sulfur compounds are known to cause acid rain, petrochemical companies attempt to remove most of them at the refinery.
  • However, some sulfur compounds are still present in the fuels and when the hydrocarbon fuels burn, the sulfur reacts with oxygen in the air.
  • This produces sulfur dioxide gas.

Balancing equation

Write a balanced equation for the reaction between sulfur and oxygen.

S(s) + O2(g) → SO2(g)

Producing acid rain

Sulfur dioxide dissolves in the water in clouds to form a weak acid, known as sulfurous acid, H2SO3.

SO2(g) + H2O(l) → H2SO3(aq)

Sulfurous acid can be oxidised by oxygen to form a strong acid, known as sulfuric acid, H2SO4.

2H2SO3(aq) + O2(g) → 2H2SO4(aq)

It is also possible to write the equations combined with only sulfuric acid being produced.

2SO2(g) + 2H2O(l) + O2(g) → 2H2SO4(aq)

Impacts of acid rain

  • If the soil becomes too acidic, plants do not grow as well. This can have an impact on carbon dioxide levels in the atmosphere, leading to global warming.
  • It also prevents crops from growing well, which can impact on food security around the world.
  • Acidity building up in lakes or rivers can kill fish and insects as well as preventing fish eggs from hatching.
  • Limestone and marble are largely composed of calcium carbonate. Buildings made from limestone or marble will react with sulfuric acid, breaking down their structure, so acid rain will increase the rate of weathering.
  • Acid rain will also increase the rate of corrosion of metals, like iron and steel, which will weaken them.

Acid rain damage

Oxides of nitrogen

Motor vehicle engines

nitrogen + oxygen → nitrogen monoxide      N2(g) + O2(g) → 2NO(g)

After the nitrogen monoxide is emitted from the vehicle’s exhaust it combines with oxygen in the air, forming nitrogen dioxide.

nitrogen monoxide + oxygen → nitrogen dioxide   2NO(g) + O2(g) → 2NO2(g)

Together, nitrogen monoxide (NO) and nitrogen dioxide (NO2) are referred to simply as NOx.

Removing SO2 and NOx from the air

  • Catalytic converters in vehicle exhaust systems convert most of the NOx into harmless nitrogen (N2).
  • Both causes of acid rain, SO2 and NOx, can be removed from the gases released by power stations and factories. This is known as scrubbing.

Learning objectives

Learning objectives

In this lesson, students will learn to:

  • explain the term dynamic equilibrium
  • explain why it takes time for a system to reach equilibrium
  • describe the conditions under which a reversible reaction can reach equilibrium.

Key definitions

Key definitions

  • closed system: when substances cannot enter or leave an observed environment, e.g., a stoppered test tube
  • dynamic equilibrium: when the forward and backward reactions in a reversible chemical reaction are occurring at the same rate
  • forward reaction: from reactants to products (the left-to-right reaction)
  • open system: a system into or from which substances can enter or leave, such as a reaction inside an open test tube
  • reverse reaction: from products to reactants (the right-to-left reaction); the reverse reaction is also known as the backward reaction

Question

The manufacture of ammonia by the Haber process involves a reversible reaction between nitrogen (from the air) and hydrogen (from natural gas) that can reach a dynamic equilibrium.

The equation for this reaction is: N2(g) + 3H2(g) ⇌ 2NH3(g)

Explain how a dynamic equilibrium is reached in the formation of ammonia.

Answer

A dynamic equilibrium is reached when the forward and backward reactions occur at the same rate and the amounts of reactants and product (nitrogen, hydrogen and ammonia) remain constant.

Exam-style question

Exam-style question

  • What is happening when a reaction reaches ‘dynamic equilibrium’?
  • Extension: Explain why a reaction producing gases which takes place in an open test tube cannot reach equilibrium.

Exam-style question

  • What is happening when a reaction reaches ‘dynamic equilibrium’?
  • Extension: Explain why reactions producing gases which take place in open test tubes cannot reach equilibrium.
  • A dynamic equilibrium occurs when the forward and backwards reactions continue at the same rate and the amount/mass/volume/percentage of reactants and the amount/mass/volume/percentage of products remain constant.
  • Extension: An equilibrium will not be achieved in an open system because any gases produced will escape so the backward reaction cannot occur.

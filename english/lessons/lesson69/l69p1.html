Lesson 69 | Overview Creating Character

A01
Read and understand a variety of texts, selecting and interpreting information, ideas and perspectives.
A02
Understand and analyse how writers use linguistic and structural devices to achieve their effects.

In this lesson, students will learn to:

  • explore how writers create character.

Students should already be familiar with:

  • identifying the writer’s intentions
  • analysing the writer’s choices.

There are no key definitions in this lesson.

Pearson Edexcel International GCSE (9–1) English Language A Student Book: pp. 214–17


  • Some students may focus on only the explicit information given about a character, or on a single piece of inferred information. This can, at best, limit their response to a character. For example, some students might focus on the single negative aspect of a character that the writer intended only as a small flaw, while ignoring a greater number of positive aspects that the writer intended would encourage the reader to be sympathetic to the character. Emphasise to students the importance of gathering a range of evidence in their response to character, regarding this as a body of evidence to be considered holistically.

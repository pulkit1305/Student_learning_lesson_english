Learning objectives

Learning objectives

In this lesson, students will learn to:

  • explore the impact of the writer’s choice of first- or third-person narration in a text
  • analyse a text written in the first person.

Key definitions

Subject vocabulary

  • first-person narrative: a narrative recounted by the speaker, writer or narrator referring to themselves using the first-person pronoun ‘I’
  • third-person narrative: a narrative in which the speaker, writer or narrator does not refer to themselves and all characters are identified using the third person pronouns ’he’, ‘she’ or ‘they’
  • narrator: a character that tells the story in a novel, play, poem or film

General vocabulary

  • wigwam: tent with a round structure or pointed roof
  • grandeur: grandness
  • Georgian: British style of architecture during the period 1714–1811
  • archaeologists: scientists who study the past by looking at historical objects and sites
  • unbiddable: will not be told what to do
  • oddly attenuated: strangely long and thin
  • mannequins: models or dummies
